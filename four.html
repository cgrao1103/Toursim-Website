Forts of Goa

Fort Aguada

Fort Aguada is a 17th-century Portuguese fort in Goa, India. It was built in 1612 to guard against Dutch and Maratha invasions. The fort is located on the Sinquerim Beach and offers breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea. It is now a popular tourist attraction.

Chapora Fort

Chapora Fort is a 17th-century fort located in North Goa. It was built by the Portuguese to guard against Maratha invasions. The fort is famous for its picturesque location on the Chapora River, and for its appearance in the Bollywood movie Dil Chahta Hai.

Terekhol Fort

Terekhol Fort is a 17th-century fort located on the banks of the Terekhol River. It was built by the Portuguese to guard against Maratha invasions. The fort has now been converted into a luxury hotel and is a popular destination for tourists.

Reis Magos Fort

Reis Magos Fort is a 16th-century fort located in North Goa. It was built by the Portuguese to guard against Dutch and Maratha invasions. The fort is famous for its beautiful architecture and its stunning views of the Arabian Sea. It has now been converted into a museum.

Cabo de Rama Fort

Cabo de Rama Fort is a 16th-century fort located in South Goa. It was built by the Portuguese to guard against Maratha invasions. The fort is situated on a cliff overlooking the Arabian Sea and offers breathtaking views of the coastline. It is now a tourist attraction.
